This project is intentionally a **local educational RAG prototype**: retrieval and generation assume a trusted workstation, plaintext Wikipedia-derived context, and no auth or abuse controls. For anything beyond demos, do **not** expose Streamlit or ad-hoc Ollama endpoints directly on the internet. Put an **API tier** behind HTTPS (reverse proxy / API gateway), add **authentication, rate limiting, and audit logging**, and treat **answers as untrusted** unless you layer human review or policy constraints (PII/copyright/abuse moderation as required by your org).

Operationally, plan for **scaling and compliance** separately from this repo: host Ollama or an equivalent inference service on **provisioned GPUs** or a managed inference product, persist Chroma or migrate to a **managed vector DB** with backups and index versioning, rebuild embeddings when models change, respect **MediaWiki/API terms** if you widen ingestion or raise request rates, and add **metrics and tracing** (latency, retrieval quality, hallucination escapes) plus an on-call playbook. For production reliability, ingest Wikipedia through **batch dumps or sanctioned APIs**, monitor rate limits and SSL trust on batch workers, and keep embedder/chat model versions pinned with reproducible ingestion pipelines.
